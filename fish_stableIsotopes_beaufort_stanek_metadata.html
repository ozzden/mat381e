Description:
Abstract:
This dataset contains isotope values of muscle tissue from fish collected in the nearshore Beaufort Sea, Alaska in summers 2017-2019.
Purpose:
Stable isotope values of carbon and nitrogen were measured for use in mixing models to assess the relative contribution of marine and terrestrial organic matter to the fish food-webs of the nearshore Beaufort Sea, Alaska.

Methodology:
Methodology_Type: Field
Methodology_Description:
Fyke nets were deployed in water ~1 m deep and up to 50 m from shore. Most fish captured in fyke nets were released live after identification, counting, and measurement every 24 h, with a subset saved for this study. Beam trawls (1 m or 3 m) were opportunistically deployed in 2017 and 2018 near the fyke net stations to ensure fish using habitat away from the shoreline were represented in this analysis.
Methodology:
Methodology_Type: Lab
Methodology_Description:
Fish were stored frozen at -20 °C prior to laboratory work. In the laboratory, each fish was thawed and measured to the nearest mm. Length was measured from the tip of the snout to the fork of the tail (fork length), or to the end of the tail for species without forked tails (total length). Following length measurement, an epaxial muscle sample was collected for stable isotope analysis and kept frozen at -20 °C. Muscle samples were freeze-dried for at least 48 hours and homogenized by grinding to a fine powder with a mortar and pestle. Approximately 1 mg of sample was weighed into tin cups. Analysis was performed at the University of Texas at Austin Marine Science Institute core facilities in Port Aransas, Texas, USA. A ThermoFisher Scientific EA-Isolink CNSOH Elemental Analyzer (Waltham, Massachusetts, USA) coupled to a ThermoFisher Scientific Delta V isotope ratio mass spectrometer in continuous-flow (He) mode was used to determine fish muscle carbon and nitrogen isotopic compositions. All values are reported in delta (Î´) notation, relative to standards VPDB for Î´13C and air for Î´15N. Calibration of Î´13C to VPDB and Î´15N to air was achieved using standards USGS-40 (Î´13C = -26.39 â€°; Î´15N = -4.52 â€°) and USGS-41a (Î´13C = 37.63 â€°; Î´15N = 47.57 â€°). Accuracy was evaluated using a casein protein standard purchased from EA Consumables (Pennsauken, New Jersey, USA). Note: lipids were not extracted from tissue samples and a lipid correction factor has not been applied to values. Additional details are available in Stanek et al 2022.

Attribute_Label: FishID
Attribute_Definition:
The unique identifier for each fish whose muscle was analyzed for stable isotope composition. The FishIDs start with a four-letter code reflecting the common name with two letters from the first word and two letters from the second word. The second part of the FishID is a three-digit number. Numbers may not be sequential as stable isotope analysis was not conducted on all fish sampled. Example: FOSC160 refers to Fourhorn (FO) Sculpin (SC) individual 160. Note: The common name for Arctic Smelt at the initiation of this study was Rainbow Smelt, consequently the code RASM is used throughout to refer to Arctic Smelt.
